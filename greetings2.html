A Note on Handshakes

When meeting someone for the first time—especially in a formal or semi-formal setting—offering or accepting a handshake is typical. British handshakes are generally firm but not aggressive, and eye contact with a polite smile shows you’re engaged and respectful. If the other person doesn’t initiate, you can wait for them to offer, or extend your hand slightly and see if they reciprocate. In more casual settings (like meeting friends of friends), a quick handshake might still happen, though it’s less mandatory compared to formal contexts.

Keep practicing until you feel comfortable using these greetings and phrases!
