Common Greetings

  • “Good morning”
    Use before noon.
  • “Good afternoon”
    Use after 12 PM until around 5 PM.
  • “Good evening”
    Use after 5 PM, typically until bedtime.
  • “Good night”
    Used as a farewell, not a greeting.
  • “Hello”
    Appropriate any time of day, a versatile greeting.
  • “Hi”
    A casual, friendly way to greet someone.
  • “Alright?”
    Common in informal settings, often means "How are you?"
  • “How's it going?”
    An informal way of saying "How are you?" asking for an update on the other person's life.

Polite Phrases

  • “Please”
    Always include this when making a request.
  • “Thank you”
    Essential for expressing gratitude.
  • “Excuse me”
    Use to politely get someone’s attention or to navigate through a crowd.
  • “I’m sorry”
    Say this when you make a mistake or need to apologize.

Example Scenarios

  • Greeting a Lecturer:
  • “Good morning, Professor. How are you?”
  • Meeting a Friend:
  • “Hi! How’s it going?”
  • Asking for Help in a Store:
  • “Excuse me, could you help me find this item, please?”

Practice Makes Perfect

Try using these phrases in your daily interactions. Whether you're speaking to a professor, a shop assistant, or a new friend, these greetings and polite expressions will help you communicate effectively and courteously.

Remember, in British culture, a little politeness goes a long way!
